《瞬息全宇宙》中母女关系的变化——从结构主义到辩证法

摘要

This paper analyzes the evolving relationship between Evelyn and her daughter Joy in Everything Everywhere All at Once. We seek to answer the following question: Why does the relationship between Evelyn and her daughter undergo such a dramatic transformation? The study analyzes such question through the theoretical lenses of structuralism and Hegelian dialectics, ultimately concluding that the root of the mother-daughter conflict lies in the failure of the Symbolic Order to fully symbolize the Real. Specifically, when facing the collapse of the symbolic order, Evelyn’s initial coping strategy - relying on ‘fantasy’ and ‘adding signifiers that slips along the signifying chain’ to suppress surplus - inevitably produces more surplus, as symbolization always generates new remnants. The excessive use of symbolization leads to the “death” of the subject. Evelyn’s resolution comes not through violently repressing surplus (her daughter’s loss of control), but through facing the surplus, and turning towards containment and symbiosis.
